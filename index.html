Engineering and Technology for the Better Good of Society

Our Mission Statement


Train the incoming engineering technology student to leverage the power of select technologies aligned to the public interest applications and targeted public works institutions.

Offer pathways to public service careers by first making students aware that such options exist early in their studies with PIT-centric course work.
Module 1: Project Management Technology for various Public Works institutions
Components
Expose students to the importance of organized planning and creating dynamic scheduling offered only with professional software technology.


Deliverable
Step-by-step learning module on using MS ProjectTM.
The module includes transparent scheduling algorithm and user-friendly interface.


Lab Component
Three virtual case studies are created and stored as an OER resource.
The studies allow creation of virtual case study per instructor’s permission.

Module 2: Technology and Ethics: “Are You Conflicted?”
Components
Conflicts between ethics in engineering and industrial practices.
Three contemporary, actual case studies of ethical conflicts in various industrial sectors.


Deliverable
Self-contained course packet consisting of videos, lecture slides.


Lab Component

Field-Demo visits to Public Work Institution will provide highly coveted experiential learning in a Public works surrounding.

Module 4: How “Open” is Open Source Content: The do’s and Don’t of using Open Source Content
Components
Meaning and types of open sources software (OSS) in engineering and technology.
Advantages and disadvantages of using OSS / Content Reliability and feasibility of the use and implementation of OSS.

Deliverable
Self-contained course packet consisting of videos, lecture slides.


Lab Component
Lab Exercises on creating mini Project assembly and use of OSS and Simulated Case Study for Evaluation.
Students’ lab reports including discussion of the three cases, appropriateness of employing OSS, and appropriate approach to OSS for satisfactory and adequate use of the OSS.

Professor Huixin Wu, M.S.

Professor Wu is the course coordinator of the Digital Computer Theory course with the department of Engineering Technology at QCC. As the course coordinator, professor Wu updates the course outline and also creates homework and exercises to complement the learning materials for the course. She also has participated in grants and has experiences in creating new teaching terminologies for engineering technology students. She was the CO-PI of the National Science Foundation (NSF) STEM grant titled “A video Lecture Library and an Interactive Systems for Computer Programming Concepts”. In addition from her teaching schedule, she is the lead of the curriculum development of TechWorks grant, and a faculty mentor of the students Undergraduate Research Project program at QCC. Professor Wu has more than ten years of experience teaching both the Digital Computer Theory course and laboratory in QCC and NYCCT.
